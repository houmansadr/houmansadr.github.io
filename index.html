Bio: I am an Associate Professor in computer science at UMass Amherst, where I joined in Fall'14 (tenured since Fall'19). I obtained my Ph.D. from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2012 and I was a postdoctoral scholar for two years at the University of Texas at Austin. Earlier, I received my M.Sc. and B.Sc. degrees from Sharif University of Technology. Research Area: My general area of research is network/AI security and privacy, and I am in particular actively investigating privacy-enhancing technologies and trustworthy AI. To that end, I design and deploy censorship circumvention systems, analyze network protocols and services (e.g., messaging apps and machine learning APIs) for privacy leakage, perform theoretical analysis to derive bounds on privacy (e.g., using game theory and information theory), and evaluate the security and privacy of emerging AI technologies.
